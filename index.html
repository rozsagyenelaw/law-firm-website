Do I need a trust or is a will sufficient?

While a will is better than nothing, a living trust offers significant advantages in California. Trusts avoid probate (saving time and money), maintain privacy, and provide better control over asset distribution. We can help you determine which option best suits your needs.

How to avoid probate in California?

The most effective ways to avoid California probate include creating a revocable living trust, using joint ownership with right of survivorship, designating beneficiaries on accounts and insurance policies, and utilizing small estate affidavits for estates under $184,500. A living trust is typically the most comprehensive solution.

When should I update my estate plan?

You should review your estate plan every 3-5 years or after major life events such as marriage, divorce, birth of children, significant asset changes, or death of a beneficiary. We offer trust amendment services to keep your plan current.

What happens if I die without an estate plan?

Without an estate plan, California's intestacy laws determine who inherits your assets. This often doesn't align with your wishes and requires costly probate proceedings. Your minor children's guardians would be chosen by the court, not you.
