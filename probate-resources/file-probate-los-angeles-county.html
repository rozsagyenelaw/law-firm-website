How to File Probate in Los Angeles County

Rozsa Gyene Updated January 2025 13 min read

Filing probate in Los Angeles County can feel overwhelming. With multiple court locations, detailed paperwork, and specific filing procedures, many executors and administrators aren't sure where to start. If you're responsible for handling a loved one's estate in Glendale, Burbank, Pasadena, or anywhere in Los Angeles County, this guide walks you through the exact process.

To file probate in Los Angeles County, you must submit a Petition for Probate (Form DE-111) along with supporting documents to the Stanley Mosk Courthouse at 111 N. Hill Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012. The filing fee is $465, and you'll need the original will, death certificate, and information about all heirs and assets.

This complete guide provides step-by-step instructions for filing probate in Los Angeles County, including forms, fees, deadlines, and court procedures.

Do You Need to File Probate in Los Angeles County?

Not every estate requires probate. Before filing, determine if probate is necessary:

You Must File Probate If:

  • The deceased owned real estate in California solely in their name (worth more than $61,500)
  • The deceased owned personal property (bank accounts, investments, etc.) worth more than $184,500
  • Assets don't have beneficiary designations or joint ownership
  • A will needs to be validated and administered

You May Avoid Probate If:

  • All assets were in a living trust
  • All accounts had payable-on-death (POD) or transfer-on-death (TOD) beneficiaries
  • Real estate was held in joint tenancy
  • The estate qualifies for the small estate affidavit (under $184,500)
  • The surviving spouse uses a Spousal Property Petition

Where to File Probate in Los Angeles County

All probate cases in Los Angeles County are filed at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse:

Stanley Mosk Courthouse
111 N. Hill Street
Los Angeles, CA 90012
Department 5 (Probate Division)

Important: Even if the deceased lived in Glendale, Burbank, Pasadena, or other LA County cities, you still file at the downtown Los Angeles courthouse. However, hearings may sometimes be held at branch locations.

Filing Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:30 AM - 4:30 PM (subject to change)

Court Contact: (213) 830-0803

Alternative Filing Options

E-Filing: Los Angeles County requires electronic filing (e-filing) for represented parties (those with attorneys). Self-represented parties may file in person or by mail.

Mail Filing: You can mail documents to the court, but in-person filing allows you to correct any errors immediately.

Required Documents for Filing Probate

Before going to the courthouse, gather these required documents:

1. Original Will and Codicils

The court requires the original will—photocopies aren't sufficient. If you only have a copy, you may need to petition to probate a lost will (more complicated).

2. Certified Copies of Death Certificate

Obtain at least 10-15 certified copies. You'll need one for the court filing and extras for banks, insurance companies, and other institutions.

3. Completed Petition for Probate (Form DE-111)

This is the main document that starts the probate process. It includes:

  • Deceased's name, date of death, and residence
  • Nominated executor (if there's a will)
  • List of all heirs and beneficiaries with addresses
  • Estimated value of estate assets
  • Whether you're requesting IAEA authority (highly recommended)

4. Duties and Liabilities of Personal Representative (Form DE-147)

This form explains the executor's responsibilities and must be acknowledged.

5. Order for Probate (Form DE-140)

A proposed order for the judge to sign at the hearing.

6. Notice of Petition to Administer Estate (Form DE-121)

This notice will be published in a local newspaper and mailed to heirs.

7. Proof of Subscribing Witness (Form DE-131) or Proof of Holographic Instrument (Form DE-135)

If there's a will, you need one of these forms to prove its validity.

Step-by-Step: Filing Probate in Los Angeles County

Step 1: Obtain Multiple Death Certificates

Order certified death certificates from:

  • The funeral home
  • Los Angeles County Registrar-Recorder/County Clerk
  • California Department of Public Health

Cost: $28 per certified copy in Los Angeles County

Step 2: Locate the Original Will

Search:

  • The deceased's home (safe, filing cabinet, desk)
  • Safe deposit box
  • With the deceased's attorney
  • The court clerk's office (some people file wills during their lifetime)

If you cannot find the original will, consult an attorney—probating a lost will requires special procedures.

Step 3: Complete Form DE-111 (Petition for Probate)

This 3-page form is the heart of your filing. Key information needed:

About the Deceased:

  • Full legal name (and any alternate names used)
  • Date of birth and date of death
  • Street address and county of residence at death
  • Whether they were married, divorced, widowed, or never married

About the Will:

  • Date the will was signed
  • Whether codicils exist
  • Who is named as executor
  • Whether the will waives bond

About Heirs and Beneficiaries:

List EVERY person who would inherit under the will OR under California intestacy law, including:

  • Full name
  • Relationship to deceased
  • Age (or state "adult")
  • Mailing address

About Estate Assets:

Provide a reasonable estimate of:

  • Total value of personal property
  • Total value of real property
  • Annual gross income from property

Requesting IAEA Authority:

Check the box requesting "full authority under the Independent Administration of Estates Act." This allows the executor to take most actions without court approval, significantly speeding up probate.

Step 4: Complete Supporting Forms

Form DE-147 (Duties and Liabilities): Read and sign this form acknowledging your responsibilities.

Form DE-140 (Order for Probate): Fill in the case caption and check boxes matching your petition.

Form DE-121 (Notice of Petition): This will be mailed to heirs and published. Complete with hearing date (leave blank—court will fill in).

Step 5: File Your Petition with the Court

Take your completed documents to the Stanley Mosk Courthouse:

  1. Go to Department 5 (Probate Division)
  2. Submit your petition package
  3. Pay the $465 filing fee (cash, check, or credit card accepted)
  4. The clerk will stamp your documents and assign a case number
  5. The clerk will schedule your hearing date (typically 30-45 days out)

Filing Fee Breakdown:

  • Probate petition: $435
  • Additional copy certification: $30
  • Total: $465 (as of 2025)

Pro Tip: Arrive early (before 10 AM) to avoid long lines.

Step 6: Publish Notice in a Newspaper

Within 10 days of filing, you must publish the Notice of Petition to Administer Estate in a newspaper of general circulation in the city where the deceased lived.

Los Angeles County Approved Newspapers:

  • Los Angeles Daily Journal
  • Los Angeles Times
  • Glendale News-Press
  • Pasadena Star-News
  • Local community newspapers

The notice must run for three consecutive weeks before the hearing. The newspaper will charge $200-400 depending on length and publication.

After publication, the newspaper provides a Proof of Publication affidavit—you'll file this with the court before the hearing.

Step 7: Mail Notice to All Heirs and Beneficiaries

At least 15 days before the hearing, mail a copy of the Notice of Petition (Form DE-121) to:

  • All beneficiaries named in the will
  • All legal heirs (even if not in the will)
  • Anyone named as executor in the will
  • The California Attorney General (if charitable gifts are involved)

Complete a Proof of Service by Mail (Form DE-120) documenting who received notice and when.

Step 8: Complete Will Validation Procedures

If there's a will, you must prove it's valid. Two options:

Option 1: Self-Proving Will

If the will includes a self-proving affidavit (signed by witnesses before a notary), simply attach it to your petition. No further proof needed.

Option 2: Witness Declaration

If the will isn't self-proving, you need a declaration from one of the witnesses who watched the deceased sign the will. Use Form DE-131 (Proof of Subscribing Witness).

If you can't locate witnesses, you may need a handwriting expert—consult an attorney.

Step 9: Attend the Probate Hearing

Arrive at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse at least 30 minutes before your scheduled hearing time. Bring:

  • Photo ID
  • Filed-stamped copies of all documents
  • Proof of Publication from the newspaper
  • Completed Proof of Service by Mail
  • Proposed Order for Probate

What Happens at the Hearing:

  1. Check in with the courtroom clerk
  2. Wait for your case to be called
  3. The judge reviews your petition
  4. The judge may ask questions about the estate
  5. If everything is in order, the judge signs the Order for Probate
  6. You're officially appointed as executor/administrator

The hearing typically lasts 5-10 minutes for uncontested cases.

Step 10: Obtain Letters Testamentary

After the hearing, request certified copies of Letters Testamentary (Form DE-150). These documents prove your authority as executor.

Cost: $30 for the first copy, $5 for each additional copy

Order at least 6-10 certified copies—you'll need them to access bank accounts, sell property, and conduct estate business.

Timeline for Filing Probate in Los Angeles County

  • Day 1: File petition at courthouse
  • Days 1-10: Publish notice in newspaper
  • Days 15-30: Notice runs for three consecutive weeks
  • Day 15: Mail notice to heirs (at least 15 days before hearing)
  • Day 30-45: Probate hearing scheduled
  • Day 45+: Receive Letters Testamentary

From filing to appointment takes approximately 6-8 weeks in Los Angeles County.

Common Filing Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not Including All Heirs

You must list every person who would inherit under the will OR under California intestacy law—even if they're estranged. Omitting someone can result in rejection.

2. Incorrect Estate Valuation

Provide realistic estimates. Wildly inaccurate values raise red flags. Don't forget to include real estate, vehicles, bank accounts, investments, and personal property.

3. Missing Publication Deadline

You have 10 days from filing to arrange newspaper publication. Missing this deadline may require rescheduling the hearing.

4. Insufficient Notice Period

Heirs must receive at least 15 days' notice before the hearing. Mail early to avoid issues.

5. Losing the Original Will

Guard the original will carefully. If it's lost, proving a copy's validity is difficult and expensive.

6. Not Requesting IAEA Authority

Always request Independent Administration authority on Form DE-111. It dramatically speeds up the probate process.

Filing Fees for Los Angeles County Probate (2025)

  • Petition for Probate: $465
  • Letters Testamentary (certified copies): $30 first copy, $5 each additional
  • Recording fee for real property: Varies by county recorder
  • Publication cost: $200-400
  • Attorney fees: Statutory (based on estate value)
  • Probate referee fees: 0.1% of appraised property value

Do You Need an Attorney to File Probate?

California law doesn't require executors to hire an attorney, but most do. Consider legal representation if:

  • The estate is worth more than $500,000
  • There's real estate to sell or transfer
  • Any beneficiary contests the will
  • The deceased owned a business
  • There are complex tax issues
  • You're uncertain about proper procedures
  • You live out of state

Los Angeles County probate courts have high volumes and complex procedures. An experienced probate attorney knows the local rules and can prevent costly mistakes.

Attorney Compensation: California uses a statutory fee schedule based on estate value (same as executor compensation). For example, an estate worth $500,000 entitles the attorney to $13,000 in fees, paid from the estate.

Special Considerations for Los Angeles County

High Court Volume

Los Angeles County has the busiest probate court in California. Expect:

  • Longer wait times for hearings
  • Strict adherence to procedures and deadlines
  • Less flexibility than smaller counties

Local Rules

LA County has specific local probate rules supplementing statewide procedures. Review the LA County Probate Court's local rules before filing.

Department Assignment

Probate cases are assigned to specific departments. After filing, all subsequent filings go to your assigned department, not the general filing window.

After Filing: Next Steps

Once you're appointed as executor, your work truly begins:

  1. Open estate bank account
  2. Notify creditors within 4 months
  3. Prepare and file inventory within 4 months
  4. File deceased's final tax returns
  5. Pay estate debts
  6. Manage estate property
  7. Petition for final distribution (after creditor period)
  8. Distribute assets to beneficiaries
  9. Close the estate

Completing probate takes 9-18 months on average in Los Angeles County.
