International Assets and Cross-Border Estate Planning

Many Arcadia residents maintain connections to their countries of origin—property in China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, or other Asian countries, bank accounts denominated in foreign currencies, business interests spanning multiple countries, or investment portfolios held in international markets. If you have assets in multiple countries, estate planning becomes significantly more complex.

A living trust can hold both U.S. and foreign assets, allowing coordinated administration of your worldwide estate. Arcadia Probate Court Information

Probate Costs for High-Value Arcadia Estates

California's statutory probate fee schedule creates enormous costs for Arcadia estates. Fees are calculated as a percentage of the gross estate value (before debts are deducted), meaning high property values trigger high probate costs even if you have significant mortgages or other debts. For typical Arcadia property values, statutory fees are:

  • $1,500,000 estate: $64,000 in statutory fees
  • $2,000,000 estate: $78,000 in statutory fees
  • $3,000,000 estate: $104,000 in statutory fees
  • $4,000,000 estate: $124,000 in statutory fees
  • $5,000,000 estate: $144,000 in statutory fees

These figures represent only the mandatory attorney and executor fees. Additional costs include court filing fees, publication costs, professional appraisals (essential for high-value Arcadia properties and required by the court), accounting fees, and potentially extraordinary fees if the estate involves international assets, business valuations, or complex litigation.

For an Arcadia estate valued at $3 million—which is not uncommon given property values—total probate costs often exceed $125,000-$150,000. This represents money that comes directly from your children's and grandchildren's inheritance, reducing the wealth you've spent a lifetime accumulating.

Timeline Delays for International Assets
