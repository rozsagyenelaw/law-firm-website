Understanding the probate process is essential for Burbank residents considering whether they need a living trust. Burbank, like all Los Angeles County communities, falls under the jurisdiction of the Los Angeles County Superior Court probate system, with most cases handled at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse in downtown Los Angeles.

The Los Angeles County Probate Court

When a Burbank resident dies without a living trust, their estate typically must go through probate at the downtown LA courthouse located at 111 North Hill Street. This means your family members will need to make multiple trips from Burbank to downtown Los Angeles—navigating traffic, finding expensive parking, and spending hours waiting in crowded courtrooms.

The probate process involves numerous steps: filing a petition with the court, notifying all heirs and beneficiaries, publishing notice to creditors in a Burbank or Los Angeles newspaper, inventorying and appraising all estate assets, paying debts and taxes, obtaining court approval for various actions, and filing a final accounting before assets can be distributed. Each step takes time and generates legal fees.

Probate Timeline and Delays

In Los Angeles County, the average probate case takes 12 to 18 months from start to finish, though cases involving entertainment industry assets, intellectual property disputes, or complex family situations can take significantly longer. During this entire period, your assets are frozen—beneficiaries cannot access inherited property, royalty and residual payments may be held in limbo, and family members may struggle financially waiting for the estate to close.

For Burbank entertainment professionals, probate delays can be particularly problematic. If you receive ongoing residuals from television shows or streaming content, or royalties from music or voice work, these payments typically cannot be distributed to your heirs until the probate case concludes. This can leave your family without the income stream they were expecting, forcing them to seek other financial support during the lengthy probate process.

The True Cost of Probate for Burbank Estates

California law establishes a statutory fee schedule for probate, calculated as a percentage of the gross estate value. For a typical Burbank home valued at $950,000, statutory fees are:

  • Attorney fees: $23,000
  • Executor fees: $23,000
  • Total statutory fees: $46,000

These figures represent only the mandatory statutory fees. Additional costs include court filing fees (approximately $435-$465), publication costs for legal notices ($300-$500), professional appraisal fees for real estate and personal property, accounting fees, and potentially bond premiums if required by the court. All told, probate costs for a typical Burbank estate commonly reach $50,000 or more—money that comes directly out of your family's inheritance.

For Burbank estates including entertainment industry assets, additional complications arise. Intellectual property may require specialized appraisals. Ongoing royalty and residual income must be tracked and reported. Production company interests may need business valuations. These complexities increase professional fees and extend the probate timeline even further.

How a Living Trust Avoids Probate

A properly funded living trust allows your entire estate to bypass probate completely. When you pass away, your successor trustee simply distributes assets according to your trust instructions—no court involvement, no downtown LA courthouse appearances, no statutory fees, no 12-18 month delays. For Burbank families, this means immediate access to inherited property, continuity of royalty and residual income, privacy (trust administration is not public record), and savings of $50,000 or more in probate costs.
