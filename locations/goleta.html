Revocable Living Trusts

A revocable living trust gives you complete control over your assets during your lifetime while providing automatic transfer to your beneficiaries upon your death. As your Goleta trust attorney, we customize your trust to address your family situation, whether you have young children, adult beneficiaries, blended families, or special needs loved ones.

Wills and Pour-Over Wills

Every comprehensive estate plan includes both a living trust and a will. A pour-over will ensures that any assets not in your trust are transferred into it upon your death. Our Goleta wills and trusts attorney prepares coordinated documents that work together seamlessly.

Powers of Attorney

A durable power of attorney allows someone you trust to manage your finances if you become incapacitated. Without this document, your family may need to establish a conservatorship through Santa Barbara County Superior Court—an expensive and time-consuming process that can be avoided with proper planning.

Advance Healthcare Directives

An advance healthcare directive (living will) ensures your medical wishes are honored and appoints someone to make healthcare decisions if you cannot. This critical document protects your family from difficult decisions during already challenging times.

Trust Administration Attorney — Goleta

When a loved one with a living trust passes away, the successor trustee must administer the trust. While this avoids probate, it still involves significant responsibilities. Our Goleta trust administration attorney guides trustees through the process, including:

  • Notifying beneficiaries of their rights
  • Marshaling trust assets
  • Obtaining asset valuations
  • Paying debts and taxes
  • Preparing trust accountings
  • Distributing assets to beneficiaries
  • Obtaining releases and closing the trust

Proper trust administration protects trustees from personal liability and ensures beneficiaries receive their inheritances promptly and correctly.
