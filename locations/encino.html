Why is a Living Trust mandatory for Encino homeowners?

In Encino, CA, where median home prices currently exceed $2,120,000, the California probate process is a massive financial liability. Without a Living Trust, an estate valued at $2.1M will trigger mandatory statutory fees of approximately $68,000 and force your heirs into a 16-month court backlog at the Van Nuys Courthouse. A trust ensures immediate private transfer of your assets, keeping your personal legacy out of the public record.

Navigating the Van Nuys Courthouse: Northwest District

If you pass away in Encino without an estate plan, your family must navigate the Los Angeles Superior Court - Northwest District. Probate and conservatorship matters for the 91316 and 91436 zip codes are primarily handled at the Van Nuys Courthouse East (6230 Sylmar Ave, Van Nuys, CA 91401).

The probate system in Van Nuys is currently facing heavy caseloads, with 12 to 18-month timelines becoming standard. Proposition 19 & Property Tax Defense

Because many Encino properties have been held for decades, they carry an extremely low tax base. Under California Proposition 19, if your heirs inherit your primary residence and do not make it their own primary residence within 12 months, the property tax will jump to current market value. On a $2.1M home, this could mean an annual tax bill increasing from $4,000 to $25,000. Our attorney-drafted Living Trusts include specific flexibility for successor trustees to help preserve your tax base for the next generation.
