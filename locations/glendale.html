Glendale Probate Court Information

Understanding the local probate process is crucial for Glendale residents considering whether they need a living trust. Glendale falls under the jurisdiction of the Los Angeles County Superior Court system, and probate cases are typically handled at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse located at 111 North Hill Street in downtown Los Angeles.

The Probate Process in Los Angeles County

When a Glendale resident passes away without a living trust, their estate typically must go through the probate process. This court-supervised procedure involves:

  • Filing a petition with the Los Angeles County Superior Court
  • Notifying all heirs and beneficiaries
  • Publishing notice to creditors in a Glendale or Los Angeles newspaper
  • Inventorying and appraising all estate assets
  • Paying debts and taxes
  • Distributing remaining assets to beneficiaries
  • Filing a final accounting with the court

Timeline and Costs

In Los Angeles County, the average probate case takes 12 to 18 months to complete, though complex estates can take significantly longer. During this time, assets are typically frozen, and beneficiaries cannot access their inheritance. This can create financial hardship for surviving family members who may need immediate access to funds for living expenses or to maintain property.

The costs of probate in California are set by statute and calculated based on the gross value of the estate (before debts are deducted). For a typical Glendale home valued at $900,000, statutory probate fees would be approximately $21,000 for the attorney and another $21,000 for the executor—a total of $42,000 in fees alone. When you add court costs, appraisal fees, accounting fees, and other expenses, the total cost often reaches 4-7% of the estate value.

How Living Trusts Avoid Probate

A properly funded living trust allows your estate to completely bypass the probate process. Assets held in your living trust are distributed to your beneficiaries according to your instructions, without court involvement, typically within weeks rather than months. This saves your family thousands of dollars in legal fees and court costs, while also providing privacy since trust administration is not a public court proceeding.

For Glendale families, this means your loved ones can avoid the stress of navigating the downtown Los Angeles courthouse system, attending multiple court hearings, and dealing with the bureaucracy of probate court. Instead, your successor trustee can administer the trust privately, efficiently, and according to your exact wishes.
