Orange County Probate Court Information

Understanding the local probate process is crucial for Orange County residents considering whether they need a living trust. Orange County has its own Superior Court system, and all probate cases are handled at the Costa Mesa Justice Complex, the dedicated probate courthouse for all of Orange County.

The Probate Process in Orange County

When an Orange County resident passes away without a living trust, their estate typically must go through the probate process. This court-supervised procedure involves:

  • Filing a petition with the Orange County Superior Court
  • Notifying all heirs and beneficiaries
  • Publishing notice to creditors in an Orange County newspaper
  • Inventorying and appraising all estate assets
  • Paying debts and taxes
  • Distributing remaining assets to beneficiaries
  • Filing a final accounting with the court

Timeline and Costs for Orange County Estates

In Orange County, the average probate case takes 12 to 18 months to complete, though complex estates can take significantly longer. During this time, assets are typically frozen, and beneficiaries cannot access their inheritance. This can create financial hardship for surviving family members who may need immediate access to funds for living expenses or to maintain valuable Orange County property.

The costs of probate in California are set by statute and calculated based on the gross value of the estate (before debts are deducted). For Orange County estates, the costs are particularly significant due to high property values:

  • $1.2 million estate: Approximately $50,000 in statutory fees ($25,000 for attorney + $25,000 for executor)
  • $2 million estate: Approximately $78,000 in statutory fees ($39,000 for attorney + $39,000 for executor)
  • $3 million estate: Approximately $110,000 in statutory fees ($55,000 for attorney + $55,000 for executor)

These figures represent only the statutory attorney and executor fees. When you add court costs, appraisal fees, accounting fees, publication costs, and other expenses, the total cost often reaches 4-7% of the estate value. For an Orange County estate valued at $2 million, total probate costs could exceed $120,000—money that should go to your family, not the probate process.
