Why is a Living Trust essential for Santa Monica residents?

With Santa Monica median home values reaching $1,600,000, nearly every residence exceeds the California probate limit. Without a Living Trust, your family will face an 18-month court process and statutory fees of over $58,000 at the Santa Monica Courthouse. A trust allows for immediate, private transfer of high-value coastal property, bypassing court backlogs and keeping your financial records off the public record.

Silicon Beach Tech Equity & Asset Integration

Santa Monica is the heart of Silicon Beach. Many of our clients are professionals at firms like Snap, Hulu, or Google who possess complex compensation packages including RSUs (Restricted Stock Units), ISOs, and NQSOs. A standard will is insufficient for these assets, as they can be frozen for months during a probate proceeding.

We specialize in Tech-Asset Estate Planning. We ensure that your brokerage accounts and equity grants are properly "funded" into your trust. This prevents a "legal freeze" on your shares, allowing your heirs to manage or liquidate stock in a timely, tax-efficient manner following your passing.

Proposition 19 & Santa Monica Real Estate

Santa Monica property has seen some of the highest appreciation rates in the country. Under California Proposition 19, if your children inherit your family home but do not make it their primary residence within one year, the property tax will be reassessed to current market value. For an Ocean Park home purchased in the 1980s, this could mean a tax jump from $4,000 to $20,000 per year.

We implement specialized trust provisions that provide the flexibility needed to keep the "Prop 13" tax base whenever legally possible. We help families plan for multi-generational residence transfers, ensuring your children can afford to keep the home they grew up in.

The Santa Monica Courthouse: Local Probate Jurisdiction

Santa Monica residents are served by the Los Angeles County Superior Court - West District. Probate and conservatorship filings are handled locally at the Santa Monica Courthouse (1725 Main St).

While the Santa Monica court is more convenient than driving to Downtown LA, the backlog is still significant. As your Santa Monica probate lawyer, we move quickly to utilize Heggstad Petitions and Small Estate procedures to minimize court time and keep your family out of the 1725 Main St. halls.
