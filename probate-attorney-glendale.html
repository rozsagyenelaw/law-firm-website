How Much Does Probate Cost in Glendale?

Understanding probate costs helps you plan and make informed decisions. In California, including Glendale, probate attorney and executor fees are set by statute based on the gross value of the estate. This means all Glendale probate attorneys charge the same statutory fee—the difference is in experience, service quality, and efficiency.

California Statutory Probate Fees

Estate Value Attorney Fee Executor Fee
First $100,000 4% ($4,000) 4% ($4,000)
Next $100,000 3% ($3,000) 3% ($3,000)
Next $800,000 2% 2%
Next $9,000,000 1% 1%
Above $10,000,000 0.5% 0.5%

Example: For a Glendale estate valued at $500,000, the probate attorney Glendale fee would be $13,000 (4% of first $100k = $4,000 + 3% of next $100k = $3,000 + 2% of remaining $300k = $6,000).

Additional Probate Costs

  • Court Filing Fees: $435-$465 to file the petition with Los Angeles Superior Court
  • Publication Costs: $150-$300 to publish legal notices in a Glendale newspaper
  • Probate Referee Fee: 0.1% of appraised asset value for independent appraisal
  • Bond Premiums: If required, typically $300-$500 annually
  • Certified Copies: $25-$50 for certified court documents

Probate Timeline in Glendale

A typical Glendale probate case takes 9-18 months from filing to final distribution. Our probate lawyer Glendale team works efficiently to complete cases as quickly as possible. The timeline generally includes:

  • Months 1-2: File petition, notify heirs, publish notice to creditors
  • Months 2-3: Attend hearing, receive Letters Testamentary
  • Months 3-6: Inventory assets, file inventory and appraisal
  • Months 4-8: Wait for 4-month creditor claim period, pay valid claims
  • Months 8-12: Prepare final accounting, petition for distribution
  • Months 12-18: Final hearing, distribute assets, close estate

Probate vs Trust: Which Do You Need?

One of the most common questions our Glendale probate attorney receives is whether someone needs probate or a trust. Understanding the difference helps you make informed decisions for both your current situation and your family's future.

When Probate is Required

Probate is the court-supervised process of administering a deceased person's estate. You likely need a probate lawyer Glendale if your loved one:

  • Owned real property solely in their name (not in a trust)
  • Had bank accounts, investments, or vehicles solely in their name exceeding $184,500
  • Did not have a living trust or their trust was not properly funded
  • Had a will but no trust (wills still go through probate)

When a Living Trust Avoids Probate

A properly funded living trust avoids probate entirely. Assets in the trust pass directly to beneficiaries without court involvement. As a Glendale probate and trusts attorney, I help families:

  • Create new living trusts to avoid future probate
  • Properly fund trusts by transferring assets into them
  • Administer trusts after the creator passes
  • Handle trust disputes and trust litigation

Glendale Probate Attorney FAQs

How much does a probate attorney cost in Glendale?

In Glendale and throughout California, probate attorney fees are set by statute based on the estate's gross value: 4% of the first $100,000, 3% of the next $100,000, 2% of the next $800,000, and 1% of the next $9 million. For a $500,000 estate, a Glendale probate attorney fee would be $13,000. All fees are paid from the estate assets, not your personal funds. We advance all costs upfront, so you pay nothing out of pocket until the estate closes.

How long does probate take in Glendale California?

Probate in Glendale typically takes 9-18 months from petition filing to final distribution. Simple estates with no disputes may complete in 9-12 months, while complex estates involving real property sales, creditor disputes, or will contests can take 18-24 months. California law requires a minimum 4-month creditor claim period. Our experienced probate lawyer Glendale team works efficiently to complete cases as quickly as possible while ensuring all legal requirements are met.

Can I avoid probate in California?

Yes! The most effective way to avoid probate is creating a properly funded living trust. Other methods include joint tenancy ownership, beneficiary designations on accounts, transfer-on-death deeds, and keeping estate value below $184,500. As a Glendale probate and trusts attorney, we help families both navigate probate for loved ones and create estate plans to avoid probate for themselves.

What does a Glendale probate lawyer do?

A Glendale probate lawyer guides families through California's probate process, including: filing the petition with probate court, notifying heirs and creditors, inventorying and appraising assets, managing estate finances, handling creditor claims, preparing tax returns, obtaining court approval for distributions, and distributing assets to beneficiaries. A probate attorney Glendale families trust also handles probate litigation including will contests, estate disputes, and executor removal.

Do I need a probate attorney or can I do it myself?

While California law allows self-representation, it's not recommended. Probate involves complex procedures, strict deadlines, and personal liability risks for executors. Mistakes can cause delays, additional costs, and legal liability. Since probate attorney fees are paid from the estate at statutory rates, you don't save money by doing it yourself—you just take on all the risk. A probate lawyer Glendale families rely on handles everything while protecting you from liability.
