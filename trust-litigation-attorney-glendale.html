Types of Trust Litigation We Handle

Our Glendale probate litigation lawyer handles all types of trust-related disputes:

Trust Contests

Trust contests challenge the validity of a trust based on:

  • Lack of Capacity: The settlor did not have mental capacity when creating or amending the trust
  • Undue Influence: Someone improperly pressured or manipulated the settlor to change the trust
  • Fraud: The settlor was deceived about the trust contents or provisions
  • Elder Abuse: Financial elder abuse in procuring trust changes
  • Forgery: The trust or amendments were forged or improperly executed
  • Failure of Trust Formalities: The trust was not properly created or executed

California law imposes strict deadlines for trust contests, typically 120 days after receiving notice of the trust. Our trust litigation attorney Glendale acts quickly to preserve your rights and gather evidence before deadlines expire.

Trustee Removal

When trustees breach their fiduciary duties or fail to properly administer the trust, beneficiaries can petition for removal. Grounds for trustee removal include:

  • Breach of fiduciary duty (self-dealing, mismanagement, favoritism)
  • Refusal to account to beneficiaries
  • Conflicts of interest
  • Hostility toward beneficiaries
  • Inability or unwillingness to administer the trust
  • Failure to follow trust terms
  • Substantial change in circumstances

Our Glendale probate litigation attorney represents beneficiaries seeking trustee removal and successor trustees taking over after removal.

Breach of Fiduciary Duty

Trustees owe beneficiaries the highest duty of loyalty and care under California law. Common breaches include:

  • Self-Dealing: Using trust assets for personal benefit
  • Conflicts of Interest: Transactions that benefit the trustee
  • Failure to Invest Prudently: Imprudent investments or failure to diversify
  • Favoritism: Treating some beneficiaries better than others
  • Failure to Account: Refusing to provide accounting of trust transactions
  • Commingling Funds: Mixing trust funds with personal funds
  • Mismanagement: Negligent handling of trust assets

Trustees who breach their fiduciary duties can be held personally liable for losses to the trust and may be required to disgorge improper gains.

Trust Accounting Disputes

Beneficiaries have the right to regular accountings showing all trust income, expenses, and distributions. Our trust litigation attorney Glendale helps beneficiaries:

  • Compel trustees to provide accountings
  • Challenge inaccurate or incomplete accountings
  • Object to improper trustee fees
  • Contest unauthorized expenses
  • Investigate suspicious transactions

Trust Interpretation Disputes

When trust language is ambiguous or parties disagree about the settlor's intent, our Glendale probate litigation lawyer seeks court instructions to clarify trust terms and resolve disputes about:

  • Distribution provisions
  • Trustee powers
  • Beneficiary rights
  • Trust administration procedures

Elder Financial Abuse Claims

California law provides enhanced remedies when someone takes advantage of an elder or dependent adult to procure trust changes. Elder abuse claims may allow recovery of attorney fees and enhanced damages beyond standard breach of fiduciary duty cases.

The California Trust Litigation Process

Trust litigation in Glendale follows a structured process:

Step 1: Case Evaluation and Evidence Gathering

Our Glendale trust litigation attorney begins with a thorough evaluation of your case, reviewing the trust document, accountings, and relevant evidence. We investigate potential claims and assess the strength of your case.

Step 2: Pre-Litigation Demands

Before filing litigation, we often send demand letters to trustees or opposing parties, outlining the legal violations and demanding corrective action. Many cases settle at this stage, avoiding the expense and delay of court proceedings.

Step 3: Filing the Petition

If pre-litigation demands fail, we file a petition with the Los Angeles County Superior Court Probate Division. The petition outlines the legal claims, requests for relief, and supporting facts.

Step 4: Discovery

During discovery, both sides exchange documents, conduct depositions, and gather evidence. Discovery in trust litigation typically includes:

  • Trust documents and all amendments
  • Financial records and accountings
  • Medical records (in capacity/undue influence cases)
  • Communications between parties
  • Expert witness reports

Step 5: Mediation

California courts typically require mediation before trial. A neutral mediator helps parties negotiate settlement. Our trust litigation attorney Glendale is an experienced negotiator who has settled hundreds of trust disputes through mediation.

Step 6: Trial

If settlement fails, the case proceeds to trial before a judge (trust cases are decided by judges, not juries). Our Glendale probate litigation attorney has extensive trial experience and presents compelling evidence to protect your rights.

Step 7: Judgment and Appeals

After trial, the court issues a judgment. The losing party may appeal to the California Court of Appeal. Our firm handles trust litigation appeals when necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is trust litigation in California?
Trust litigation encompasses legal disputes involving trusts, including trust contests, trustee removal, breach of fiduciary duty claims, trust interpretation disputes, and accounting disputes. In Glendale and throughout California, trust litigation typically occurs when beneficiaries believe a trustee has mismanaged trust assets, engaged in self-dealing, failed to distribute assets properly, or when there are questions about the validity of the trust itself. Our Glendale trust litigation attorney represents beneficiaries seeking to protect their inheritance and trustees defending against claims.
What are grounds to contest a trust in California?
California law allows trust contests based on lack of capacity (the settlor did not have mental capacity when creating or amending the trust), undue influence (someone improperly pressured the settlor to change the trust), fraud (the settlor was deceived about the trust contents), forgery, elder abuse, or failure to comply with California trust formalities. Trust contests must be filed within strict deadlines, typically 120 days after receiving notice of the trust. Our Glendale probate litigation attorney evaluates potential trust contest claims and represents clients in trust litigation proceedings.
How do I remove a trustee in California?
Trustees can be removed by court order for breach of trust, refusal to follow trust terms, conflicts of interest, self-dealing, failure to account to beneficiaries, mismanagement of trust assets, hostility toward beneficiaries that impairs administration, or substantial change in circumstances. To remove a trustee, beneficiaries must file a petition in probate court with evidence of the trustee's misconduct or unfitness. Our trust litigation attorney Glendale represents beneficiaries seeking trustee removal and helps successor trustees take over trust administration after removal.
What is breach of fiduciary duty in trust cases?
Breach of fiduciary duty occurs when a trustee fails to fulfill their legal obligations to beneficiaries, including the duties of loyalty, care, impartiality, and to follow trust terms. Common breaches include self-dealing (using trust assets for personal benefit), failing to invest prudently, favoring one beneficiary over others, commingling trust and personal funds, failing to keep beneficiaries informed, and refusing to provide accountings. Trustees who breach their fiduciary duties can be held personally liable for losses to the trust and may be required to return improper gains. Our Glendale trust litigation attorney pursues breach of fiduciary duty claims on behalf of injured beneficiaries.
How long does trust litigation take in California?
Trust litigation in Glendale and Los Angeles County typically takes 12 to 24 months from filing the petition to trial, though settlement often occurs sooner. Simple trustee removal cases may resolve in 6-9 months, while complex trust contests involving undue influence or capacity can take 2-3 years. Many trust disputes settle through mediation before trial, which can significantly reduce time and costs. The timeline depends on court calendar, complexity of issues, number of parties, and willingness to settle. Our Glendale probate litigation lawyer works efficiently to resolve cases while protecting clients' interests.

Trust litigation costs vary widely based on case complexity. Simple accounting disputes or uncontested trustee removals may cost $10,000-$25,000 in attorney fees. Complex trust contests or breach of fiduciary duty cases can cost $50,000-$200,000+ through trial.
