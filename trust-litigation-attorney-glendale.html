When You Need a Trust Litigation Lawyer in Glendale

Trust litigation becomes necessary when there are disputes about how a trust should be interpreted, administered, or distributed. As your Glendale trust litigation attorney, I understand that these disputes often arise during already difficult times—typically after the loss of a loved one when emotions are high and family relationships are strained.

You may need a Glendale trust litigation lawyer when you suspect or have evidence of trustee misconduct, breach of fiduciary duty, or when you believe a trust was created under undue influence or when the trustor lacked mental capacity. Trust disputes can also arise when trustees fail to provide accountings, make improper distributions, self-deal with trust assets, or simply refuse to communicate with beneficiaries.

Common situations that require the intervention of a trust litigation lawyer include:

  • Breach of Fiduciary Duty: When a trustee fails to act in the best interests of beneficiaries, mismanages assets, or engages in self-dealing transactions
  • Trustee Misconduct: Including theft, fraud, failure to account, improper investments, or refusing to make distributions
  • Trust Contests: Challenging the validity of a trust based on lack of capacity, undue influence, fraud, or improper execution
  • Interpretation Disputes: When beneficiaries and trustees disagree about the meaning of trust provisions
  • Removal Proceedings: When a trustee must be removed for cause and replaced with a suitable successor

California law provides significant protections for trust beneficiaries, and as your trust dispute attorney, I work to ensure those rights are fully enforced. The sooner you act, the better—delay can result in dissipated assets, destroyed evidence, or missed statute of limitations deadlines.

Types of Trust Disputes We Handle

As an experienced Glendale trust litigation attorney, I handle a wide range of trust and estate disputes. Each case is unique, but they all share one common element: the need for skilled legal representation to protect your rights and interests.

Trustee Removal

When trustees fail to fulfill their duties, beneficiaries can petition for removal under California Probate Code Section 15642. As a trustee removal attorney Glendale, I help beneficiaries document misconduct and present compelling cases for removal to the Los Angeles Superior Court.

Breach of Fiduciary Duty

Trustees owe the highest duty of loyalty and care to beneficiaries. As a breach of fiduciary duty lawyer, I pursue claims against trustees who engage in self-dealing, fail to account, make imprudent investments, or otherwise violate their legal obligations.

Trust Contests & Challenges

If you believe a trust was created through fraud, undue influence, or when the trustor lacked mental capacity, you may have grounds to contest its validity. We investigate the circumstances and build strong cases for invalidation when warranted.

Undue Influence Claims

When someone in a position of trust manipulates a vulnerable person into changing their estate plan, it constitutes undue influence. We pursue claims to restore the trustor's true intentions and protect rightful beneficiaries.

Elder Abuse & Financial Exploitation

Financial abuse of elders is unfortunately common and often involves manipulation of trusts and estate plans. We pursue claims under California's Elder Abuse Act, which provides enhanced remedies including attorney fees and punitive damages.

Accounting Disputes

Beneficiaries have the right to receive accountings from trustees. When trustees fail to account or provide incomplete or fraudulent accountings, we petition the court to compel proper disclosure and surcharge trustees for any losses.

The Trust Litigation Process

Trust litigation in California follows a structured process through the Probate Division of the Los Angeles Superior Court. Understanding this process helps you make informed decisions about your case:

Litigation Timeline

  • Case Evaluation: We thoroughly review trust documents, gather evidence, interview witnesses, and assess the strength of your claims or defenses
  • Pre-Litigation Negotiation: Many disputes can be resolved without court intervention through skilled negotiation, saving time and legal fees
  • Petition Filing: When litigation is necessary, we file appropriate petitions with the Los Angeles Superior Court Probate Division
  • Discovery: We conduct thorough discovery including document requests, interrogatories, depositions, and subpoenas to build your case
  • Mediation: California courts often require mediation before trial, providing another opportunity for resolution
  • Trial: If settlement isn't possible, we present your case at trial with thorough preparation and aggressive advocacy

Remedies Available in Trust Litigation

Depending on the nature of your case, various remedies may be available:

  • Trustee Removal and Replacement: Removing a problematic trustee and appointing a suitable successor
  • Surcharge: Holding trustees personally liable for losses caused by their misconduct
  • Trust Modification or Termination: Changing trust terms when circumstances warrant
  • Accounting and Disclosure: Compelling trustees to provide complete financial information
  • Damages: Recovering financial losses caused by breach of fiduciary duty
  • Attorney Fees: In some cases, attorney fees can be recovered from the trust or opposing party

Trust Litigation FAQs

How much does a trust litigation attorney cost in Glendale?

Trust litigation attorneys in Glendale typically charge hourly rates ranging from $350 to $600 per hour, depending on experience and case complexity. Unlike probate administration, trust litigation fees are not set by statute. Some cases may be handled on a contingency basis if there's a significant financial recovery expected. Additionally, in certain cases, attorney fees may be recoverable from the trust itself or from an opposing party who engaged in misconduct. During your free consultation, we'll discuss fee arrangements that work for your situation and provide a realistic assessment of potential costs based on the specific circumstances of your case.

How long does trust litigation take in California?

Trust litigation in California typically takes 12 to 24 months from filing to resolution, though complex cases involving multiple parties, extensive discovery, or appeals can take longer. The timeline depends on several factors including case complexity, court schedules at the Los Angeles Superior Court (which handles Glendale cases), whether the case settles or goes to trial, and the responsiveness of opposing parties. Many trust disputes settle through mediation before trial, which can significantly reduce the timeline and costs. Emergency matters, such as requests to suspend a trustee's powers, can be heard on an expedited basis when assets are at immediate risk.

Can I remove a trustee in Glendale?

Yes, you can petition the Los Angeles Superior Court to remove a trustee under California Probate Code Section 15642. Grounds for removal include: breach of fiduciary duty, failure to provide accountings, conflicts of interest, incapacity or unfitness to serve, persistent failure to administer the trust properly, excessive compensation, failure to cooperate with co-trustees, and hostility toward beneficiaries that impairs administration. As a breach of fiduciary duty lawyer experienced in trustee removal, we help beneficiaries build strong cases for removal when trustees fail to fulfill their legal obligations. The court can also suspend a trustee's powers pending the outcome of removal proceedings if assets are at risk.

What is breach of fiduciary duty in trust litigation?

Breach of fiduciary duty occurs when a trustee fails to act in the best interests of trust beneficiaries. This includes self-dealing (using trust assets for personal benefit), commingling trust assets with personal funds, failing to provide accountings, making imprudent investments, distributing assets unfairly among beneficiaries, or showing favoritism. A breach of fiduciary duty lawyer can help you recover damages, remove the trustee, and surcharge the trustee for losses caused by their misconduct.

What are grounds for contesting a trust in California?

Grounds for contesting a trust in California include: lack of mental capacity (the trustor didn't understand what they were signing), undue influence (someone manipulated the trustor into creating or changing the trust), fraud or forgery, improper execution (trust wasn't signed correctly or lacked required witnesses), and revocation (a later trust or amendment supersedes it). Trust contests must typically be filed within 120 days of receiving notice of the trust administration.

Where are Glendale trust litigation cases filed?

Trust litigation cases for Glendale residents are filed at the Los Angeles Superior Court, Stanley Mosk Courthouse, located at 111 N. Hill Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012.
