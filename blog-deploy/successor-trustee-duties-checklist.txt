7:T3b74,<p>You've become the successor trustee of a California living trust. Whether the trust creator has passed away or become incapacitated, you now have legal responsibilities to fulfill.</p>
<p>This comprehensive checklist will guide you through every step of trust administration.</p>
<h2>PHASE 1: IMMEDIATE STEPS (Days 1-14)</h2>
<h3>Gather Essential Documents</h3>
<p>‚òê Locate the original trust document</p>
<p>‚òê Find all trust amendments (if any)</p>
<p>‚òê Obtain certified death certificates (order 10-15 copies)</p>
<p>‚òê Locate the deceased's will (pour-over will)</p>
<p>‚òê Find previous tax returns (last 3 years)</p>
<p>‚òê Gather Social Security information</p>
<p>‚òê Locate life insurance policies</p>
<p>‚òê Find retirement account statements</p>
<p>‚òê Gather bank and investment account statements</p>
<p>‚òê Locate real estate deeds</p>
<p>‚òê Find vehicle titles</p>
<p>‚òê Gather business ownership documents</p>
<h3>Secure Assets Immediately</h3>
<p>‚òê Secure the residence (change locks if needed)</p>
<p>‚òê Secure vehicles</p>
<p>‚òê Collect mail and watch for bills</p>
<p>‚òê Locate and secure valuables (jewelry, art, collectibles)</p>
<p>‚òê Secure important documents</p>
<p>‚òê Notify home insurance company</p>
<p>‚òê Continue paying essential bills (mortgage, utilities, insurance)</p>
<p>‚òê Do NOT distribute any assets yet</p>
<h3>Notify Key Parties</h3>
<p>‚òê Contact an estate planning attorney</p>
<p>‚òê Contact a CPA or tax professional</p>
<p>‚òê Notify the post office if needed</p>
<p>‚òê Notify Social Security Administration</p>
<p>‚òê Notify pension/retirement plan administrators</p>
<p>‚òê Notify life insurance companies</p>
<p>‚òê Notify employer (if deceased was employed)</p>
<h2>PHASE 2: ESTABLISH YOUR AUTHORITY (Days 7-30)</h2>
<h3>Obtain Tax Identification Number</h3>
<p>‚òê Apply for trust EIN from IRS (IRS.gov or Form SS-4)</p>
<p>‚òê Do NOT use the deceased's Social Security number going forward</p>
<h3>Open Trust Bank Account</h3>
<p>‚òê Open bank account in trust name using EIN</p>
<p>‚òê Deposit any checks payable to deceased or trust</p>
<p>‚òê Transfer funds from deceased's accounts (as institutions allow)</p>
<h3>Establish Your Trustee Authority</h3>
<p>‚òê Prepare trustee certification/affidavit of trust</p>
<p>‚òê Provide death certificate and trust certification to financial institutions</p>
<p>‚òê Retitle accounts into trust name (as successor trustee)</p>
<p>‚òê Notify investment companies of trustee change</p>
<p>‚òê Record affidavit of death of trustee (for real property)</p>
<h2>PHASE 3: REQUIRED NOTICES (Days 1-60)</h2>
<h3>Send Statutory Notice to Beneficiaries and Heirs</h3>
<p><strong>DEADLINE: Within 60 days of death or becoming trustee</strong></p>
<p>‚òê Identify all trust beneficiaries</p>
<p>‚òê Identify all legal heirs (even if not beneficiaries)</p>
<p>‚òê Prepare Probate Code ¬ß16061.7 notice including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Trust name and date</li>
<li>Trustee's name and contact information</li>
<li>Statement that trust is now irrevocable</li>
<li>Right to receive copy of trust terms</li>
<li>120-day deadline to contest trust</li>
</ul>
<p>‚òê Send notice via certified mail</p>
<p>‚òê Keep copies of notices and mailing receipts</p>
<p>‚òê Document the date each notice was sent</p>
<h3>Other Required Notices</h3>
<p>‚òê Notify known creditors (recommended)</p>
<p>‚òê Notify government agencies receiving benefits</p>
<h2>PHASE 4: INVENTORY AND VALUATION (Days 14-60)</h2>
<h3>Create Complete Asset Inventory</h3>
<p><strong>Real Property:</strong></p>
<p>‚òê List all real estate owned by trust</p>
<p>‚òê Obtain date-of-death appraisals</p>
<p>‚òê Note any mortgages or liens</p>
<p>‚òê Determine current rental income (if applicable)</p>
<p><strong>Financial Accounts:</strong></p>
<p>‚òê List all bank accounts with balances</p>
<p>‚òê List all investment/brokerage accounts</p>
<p>‚òê List all retirement accounts (IRA, 401k, etc.)</p>
<p>‚òê Document date-of-death values</p>
<p><strong>Life Insurance:</strong></p>
<p>‚òê List all policies</p>
<p>‚òê Determine beneficiaries (trust or individuals)</p>
<p>‚òê File death claims</p>
<p><strong>Personal Property:</strong></p>
<p>‚òê Inventory vehicles with values</p>
<p>‚òê Inventory valuable personal property</p>
<p>‚òê Obtain appraisals for significant items</p>
<p>‚òê Document household contents</p>
<p><strong>Business Interests:</strong></p>
<p>‚òê List any business ownership</p>
<p>‚òê Obtain business valuations</p>
<p>‚òê Identify business debts and obligations</p>
<p><strong>Other Assets:</strong></p>
<p>‚òê Digital assets and accounts</p>
<p>‚òê Intellectual property</p>
<p>‚òê Money owed to deceased</p>
<p>‚òê Tax refunds expected</p>
<h3>Create Liability List</h3>
<p>‚òê Outstanding mortgages</p>
<p>‚òê Credit card balances</p>
<p>‚òê Medical bills</p>
<p>‚òê Funeral expenses</p>
<p>‚òê Outstanding loans</p>
<p>‚òê Taxes owed</p>
<p>‚òê Other debts</p>
<h2>PHASE 5: ADMINISTRATION (Months 1-6)</h2>
<h3>Ongoing Asset Management</h3>
<p>‚òê Collect income (rents, dividends, interest)</p>
<p>‚òê Pay ongoing expenses from trust account</p>
<p>‚òê Maintain insurance coverage</p>
<p>‚òê Make necessary repairs to property</p>
<p>‚òê Manage investments prudently</p>
<p>‚òê Keep all receipts and records</p>
<h3>Handle Debts and Claims</h3>
<p>‚òê Review all debts for validity</p>
<p>‚òê Pay legitimate debts from trust funds</p>
<p>‚òê Negotiate settlements if appropriate</p>
<p>‚òê Document all payments</p>
<h3>Tax Obligations</h3>
<p>‚òê File deceased's final federal income tax return (Form 1040)</p>
<p>‚òê File deceased's final California income tax return (Form 540)</p>
<p>‚òê File trust income tax returns (Form 1041 federal, Form 541 California)</p>
<p>‚òê Pay any taxes owed</p>
<p>‚òê File estate tax return if required (estates over $13.61 million)</p>
<p>‚òê Consider stepped-up basis for assets</p>
<h3>Maintain Records</h3>
<p>‚òê Keep detailed records of all transactions</p>
<p>‚òê Save all receipts and invoices</p>
<p>‚òê Document time spent on administration</p>
<p>‚òê Keep copies of all correspondence</p>
<p>‚òê Maintain separate trust accounting</p>
<h2>PHASE 6: DISTRIBUTION PREPARATION (Months 4-6)</h2>
<h3>Wait for Contest Period</h3>
<p>‚òê Wait until 120-day contest period expires</p>
<p>‚òê Confirm no trust contests have been filed</p>
<p>‚òê Resolve any disputes before distributing</p>
<h3>Prepare for Distribution</h3>
<p>‚òê Confirm all debts are paid</p>
<p>‚òê Confirm all taxes are filed and paid</p>
<p>‚òê Calculate each beneficiary's share</p>
<p>‚òê Determine how to divide specific items</p>
<p>‚òê Prepare deeds for real property transfers</p>
<p>‚òê Prepare assignment documents for other assets</p>
<h3>Consider Sub-Trusts</h3>
<p>‚òê Determine if sub-trusts must be created (for minors, etc.)</p>
<p>‚òê Prepare sub-trust documentation</p>
<p>‚òê Fund sub-trusts appropriately</p>
<h2>PHASE 7: DISTRIBUTION (Months 5-12)</h2>
<h3>Distribute Assets</h3>
<p>‚òê Prepare and record trustee's deeds for real property</p>
<p>‚òê Transfer investment accounts to beneficiaries</p>
<p>‚òê Distribute cash to beneficiaries</p>
<p>‚òê Transfer vehicles (DMV title transfer)</p>
<p>‚òê Deliver personal property items</p>
<p>‚òê Distribute any remaining specific gifts</p>
<h3>Document Distributions</h3>
<p>‚òê Obtain signed receipts from all beneficiaries</p>
<p>‚òê Document what was distributed to whom</p>
<p>‚òê Keep copies of all transfer documents</p>
<h2>PHASE 8: CLOSING (Final Month)</h2>
<h3>Final Accounting</h3>
<p>‚òê Prepare final accounting showing:</p>
<ul>
<li>All assets at beginning</li>
<li>All income received</li>
<li>All expenses paid</li>
<li>All distributions made</li>
<li>Final balance (should be zero or near zero)</li>
</ul>
<p>‚òê Provide accounting to beneficiaries</p>
<p>‚òê Obtain approval or releases if possible</p>
<h3>Close the Trust</h3>
<p>‚òê File final trust tax returns</p>
<p>‚òê Close trust bank accounts</p>
<p>‚òê Distribute any remaining funds</p>
<p>‚òê Prepare trust termination document (if required)</p>

<p>‚òê Retain all records for at least 5 years</p>
<p>‚òê Store records securely</p>
<p>‚òê Inform beneficiaries where records are kept</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>What is the first thing I should do as successor trustee in California?</h3>
<p>The first priority is locating the original trust document and all amendments, then obtaining 10-15 certified death certificates. Within the first two weeks, you should also secure all trust property (including changing locks if necessary), review the trust thoroughly to understand your duties and the distribution provisions, and contact key parties including an estate planning attorney. Do not distribute any assets yet. These immediate steps establish your foundation for proper trust administration and help you avoid costly mistakes later.</p>
<h3>What is the 60-day notice requirement for California trustees?</h3>
<p>California Probate Code Section 16061.7 requires successor trustees to send written notice to all trust beneficiaries and legal heirs within 60 days of the trustor's death or within 60 days of accepting your role as trustee. The notice must include the trust name and date, your name and contact information as trustee, a statement that the trust is now irrevocable, notification of the right to receive a copy of the trust terms, and information about the 120-day deadline to contest the trust. Send this notice via certified mail and keep copies of all notices and mailing receipts.</p>
<h3>Can I be removed as successor trustee?</h3>
<p>Yes, beneficiaries can petition the court to remove you as successor trustee if you breach your fiduciary duties. Common grounds for removal include failure to provide accountings when requested, self-dealing or conflicts of interest, mismanagement of trust assets, failure to communicate with beneficiaries, unreasonable delays in administration, commingling trust funds with personal funds, or favoring one beneficiary over others. To protect yourself from removal, document everything, communicate regularly with beneficiaries, treat all beneficiaries impartially, and consider hiring an attorney to guide you through the administration process.</p>
<h3>How do I distribute trust assets to beneficiaries?</h3>
<p>Before distributing trust assets, you must wait until the 120-day contest period expires, confirm all debts and taxes are paid, and ensure you've maintained sufficient reserves for unknown claims. Review the trust's distribution provisions carefully to understand who gets what and whether any conditions apply. You can distribute assets in-kind (transferring actual property like houses or stocks), as cash (selling assets and distributing proceeds), or a combination. Always obtain signed receipts from beneficiaries acknowledging what they received, and prepare releases protecting you from future liability before making final distributions.</p>
<h3>What tax returns do I need to file as successor trustee?</h3>
<p>As successor trustee, you're responsible for filing several tax returns. File the deceased's final federal income tax return (Form 1040) and California return (Form 540) for January 1 through the date of death, due April 15 of the following year. You must also file trust income tax returns (Form 1041 federal and Form 541 California) annually if the trust has $600 or more in income or any taxable income. If the estate exceeds $13.61 million (2024), you'll need to file a federal estate tax return (Form 706) within 9 months of death. Consider hiring a CPA to handle these filings and ensure you don't miss important tax elections.</p>
