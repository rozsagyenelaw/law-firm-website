Why Entertainment Professionals Need Specialized Estate Planning

The entertainment industry creates unique wealth that most estate planning attorneys simply don't understand. If you've spent your career at Disney, Warner Bros., Nickelodeon, or any of the studios that call Burbank home, your assets likely include:

Residuals: The Gift That Keeps Giving

Residuals are ongoing payments to performers, writers, and directors when their work is reused—through streaming, syndication, home video, or international distribution. For many Burbank entertainment professionals, residuals represent a significant portion of their lifetime earnings.

Consider these scenarios common in Burbank:

  • The veteran animator who worked on classic Disney films that still generate streaming revenue decades later
  • The TV writer whose episodes of a Warner Bros. sitcom continue to air in syndication
  • The voice actor whose character appears in Nickelodeon reruns, video games, and merchandise
  • The session musician who played on film scores that are still licensed for commercials and trailers

These residual streams can continue for 30, 40, even 50+ years after the original work was created. They're not just income—they're assets that need to be properly managed and transferred.

Intellectual Property Rights

Many Burbank creatives also own intellectual property outright:

  • Scripts and screenplays (produced or unproduced)
  • Music compositions and song catalogs
  • Original characters and concepts
  • Books, articles, and written works
  • Photography and visual art
  • Podcast content and digital media

Under copyright law, these rights persist for 70 years after the creator's death. That's 70 years of potential licensing revenue, royalty payments, and creative control decisions that someone will need to manage.

Union Benefits and Pensions

SAG-AFTRA and WGA members have access to pension and health plans that require specific beneficiary designations. These plans don't automatically follow your will or trust—they require separate paperwork that must be coordinated with your overall estate plan.

Did You Know? SAG-AFTRA pension benefits can be worth hundreds of thousands of dollars over a beneficiary's lifetime. But if you don't properly designate beneficiaries—and coordinate those designations with your trust—your family could face delays, disputes, or unintended distributions.

The Probate Trap: What Happens Without a Trust

When an entertainment professional dies without a living trust, their estate must go through probate at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse in downtown Los Angeles. For Burbank residents, this creates a perfect storm of problems:

Your Residuals Get Frozen

Without a trust, no one has legal authority to receive your residual payments. The unions and studios can't just send checks to your spouse or children—they need court documentation proving who has the right to receive the funds.

During the 18-24 months (or longer) that probate takes in LA County:

  • Residual checks may be held by the studio or union
  • Your family cannot access this income stream
  • Bills, mortgages, and living expenses continue
  • Your beneficiaries may face financial hardship

For families who depend on residual income—and many Burbank families do—this freeze can be devastating.

Your Intellectual Property Is in Limbo

Who can license your song? Who can approve the use of your screenplay? Who can negotiate deals involving your creative work?

During probate, these decisions get complicated. The court-appointed executor may not understand your creative work or share your vision for how it should be used. Important licensing opportunities may be missed. Your creative legacy may be mismanaged.

The Costs Are Substantial

California probate fees are based on gross estate value. For a typical Burbank professional with a home and accumulated assets:

$750,000 $37,000

These fees come directly out of the estate—money that should go to your family.

How a Living Trust Protects Your Creative Legacy

A properly structured living trust solves these problems by ensuring seamless transfer of your assets—including your creative income streams—without court involvement.

Continuous Residual Payments

When your trust is the designated recipient of your residuals, payments continue flowing without interruption. Your successor trustee simply provides the union or studio with documentation of their authority, and checks continue being issued—now payable to the trust for distribution to your beneficiaries.

No probate. No freeze. No 18-24 month wait.

Managed Intellectual Property

Your trust document can include specific provisions for managing your intellectual property:

  • Designated IP manager: Name someone who understands your creative work and can make licensing decisions aligned with your values
  • Licensing guidelines: Specify what types of uses you approve or prohibit (for example: "My music may not be licensed for political advertising")
  • Revenue distribution: Determine how royalties should be split among beneficiaries
  • Creative control provisions: Protect the integrity of your work from modifications you wouldn't approve

Coordinated Benefits

A comprehensive estate plan ensures your union pensions, health benefits, and other employment-related assets are properly coordinated with your trust. Beneficiary designations are reviewed and updated. Potential conflicts are identified and resolved.

Estate Planning for Different Entertainment Careers

Not all entertainment careers are alike, and estate planning needs vary based on your specific situation.

Animators and Visual Artists

Burbank is home to Disney Animation, Warner Bros. Animation, Nickelodeon Animation, and countless smaller studios. If you're an animator:

  • Your residuals from animated features and TV shows need trust protection
  • Personal artwork and side projects may be valuable IP
  • Digital art files and archives should be addressed in your plan
  • Union pension and health benefits require beneficiary coordination

Writers (WGA Members)

Writers at Warner Bros., Disney, and other Burbank studios have unique considerations:

  • WGA residuals for produced work need ongoing management
  • Unproduced scripts may have future value
  • Separated rights (publication, sequel rights) require specific treatment
  • WGA pension benefits have specific beneficiary requirements

Actors and Voice Performers

Whether you're a principal performer or a voice actor recording in studios throughout the Media District:

  • SAG-AFTRA residuals can continue for decades
  • Commercial residuals require separate tracking
  • Voice performance rights for characters may have ongoing value
  • Health and pension benefits need proper beneficiary designations

Musicians and Composers

Burbank's recording industry creates additional estate planning needs:

  • Song copyrights and publishing rights need protection
  • AFM (American Federation of Musicians) residuals apply to recorded work
  • Master recording ownership may be complex
  • Performance royalties (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC) require beneficiary updates

The Gig Economy Creative

Many Burbank creatives work as independent contractors, moving between projects and studios. This creates additional complexity:

  • Multiple residual streams from different productions
  • Varying union affiliations and benefit plans
  • Personal projects and side businesses
  • Irregular income that makes planning challenging

For gig-based entertainment professionals in the Media District and throughout Burbank, a living trust provides stability and organization for assets that might otherwise be scattered across multiple accounts, unions, and payment sources.

Real Example: A Burbank voice actor had residuals flowing from three different studios, AFM payments for session work, and a home voice-over studio generating independent income. By placing all these income streams under a single trust, we created a unified plan that ensures her family can manage everything efficiently—no hunting for scattered accounts or missing residual checks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a living trust hold my SAG-AFTRA or WGA residuals?

Yes, a properly structured living trust can hold your rights to receive SAG-AFTRA and WGA residuals. While the union membership itself cannot be transferred, your right to receive residual payments can be assigned to your trust. This ensures that when you pass away, residuals continue flowing to your beneficiaries without going through probate. Your trust becomes the designated recipient, and your successor trustee manages distributions according to your wishes.

What happens to my residuals if I die without a trust?

Without a trust, your residuals become part of your probate estate. This means your family cannot access these funds until probate is complete—typically 18-24 months in Los Angeles County. During this time, residual checks may be held by the union or studio pending court appointment of an executor. Your family loses access to income they may depend on, and a significant portion of the residuals may go toward probate fees rather than your heirs.

How do I protect my intellectual property rights in my estate plan?

Intellectual property—including copyrights, scripts, music compositions, and creative works—should be specifically addressed in your living trust. Your trust document should identify your IP assets, designate who will manage them after your death, specify how royalties should be distributed, and include provisions for licensing decisions. An experienced entertainment estate planning attorney can draft provisions that protect both the economic value and creative integrity of your work.

Do entertainment professionals need special estate planning?

Yes, entertainment professionals have unique estate planning needs that standard attorneys often overlook. Your estate likely includes irregular income streams (residuals, royalties, residual bonuses), intellectual property rights, union benefits and pension plans, and potentially complex contractual obligations. A trust attorney experienced with entertainment clients understands how to structure your plan to address these assets and ensure continuous income flow to your family.
