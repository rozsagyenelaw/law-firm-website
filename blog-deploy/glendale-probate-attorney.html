Frequently Asked Questions from Glendale Clients

Do I need a probate attorney in Glendale?

While you can legally represent yourself in California probate, most people find the process overwhelming. Between strict deadlines, complex paperwork, and personal liability risks, having an experienced attorney protects you and makes the process much smoother.

How long does probate take in Los Angeles County?

Most probate cases take 12-18 months from start to finish. Complex estates with litigation, tax issues, or real estate sales can take 2-3 years or longer.

What if my loved one had a will?

Having a will makes probate smoother because it names an executor and specifies who gets what. But the will must still go through probate to be validated and executed. Only a living trust avoids probate entirely.

What if my loved one didn't have a will?

When someone dies without a will (intestate), California's intestate succession laws determine who inherits. The process is similar to probate with a will, but the court appoints an administrator rather than an executor.

Can you help if I live out of state?

Yes. Many of my clients live outside California. I can handle most communication by phone, email, and video conference. You'll need to sign some documents (which I can send electronically), but you typically don't need to travel to California.

What if the family is fighting?

Family disputes are unfortunately common in probate. I help families navigate disagreements, mediate when possible, and litigate when necessary. My goal is always to resolve conflicts efficiently while minimizing damage to family relationships.

How much do you charge?

California law sets statutory probate fees based on the estate's value. I'll explain the exact fees for your case during your free consultation. The fees are paid from the estate, not out of your pocket.

Do you offer a free consultation?

Yes. I offer a free initial consultation where we'll discuss your situation, review any documents you have, and I'll explain your options. No obligation, no pressure.
