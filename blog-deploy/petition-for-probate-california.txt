7:T4d48,<p>The Petition for Probate (Form DE-111) is the legal document that initiates probate proceedings in California. This single form opens the probate case, requests appointment as executor or administrator, and sets the entire probate process in motion.</p>
<p>As a Glendale probate attorney who files dozens of these petitions every year, I know that properly completing Form DE-111 is critical‚Äîerrors or omissions can result in the petition being rejected, delaying the entire probate by weeks or months.</p>
<p>This comprehensive guide explains everything about filing a Petition for Probate in California: who can file, required information, step-by-step form instructions, supporting documents needed, filing procedures, the court hearing, and what happens after the petition is granted.</p>
<h2>What Is a Petition for Probate?</h2>
<p><strong>Judicial Council Form DE-111:</strong> Petition for Probate</p>
<p><strong>Purpose:</strong> Formal request to probate court to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Open probate case</li>
<li>Admit will to probate (if one exists)</li>
<li>Appoint executor (if will names one) or administrator (if no will)</li>
<li>Grant authority to administer estate</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>This is the first step</strong> in formal California probate.</p>
<h3>When Petition for Probate Is Required</h3>
<p><strong>Required when:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Decedent died with assets in their individual name</li>
<li>Total probate assets exceed $184,500</li>
<li>Real property requires probate transfer</li>
<li>No small estate procedures available</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Not required when:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Estate qualifies for small estate affidavit (under $184,500)</li>
<li>All assets in living trust</li>
<li>All assets pass by beneficiary designation or joint tenancy</li>
</ul>
<h2>Who Can File Petition for Probate?</h2>
<p><strong>California Probate Code Sections 8000-8005</strong></p>
<h3>Priority for Appointment</h3>
<p><strong>If will exists:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Executor named in will</strong> (first priority)</li>
<li><strong>Other person entitled</strong> under will</li>
<li><strong>Public administrator</strong></li>
</ol>
<p><strong>If no will (intestate):</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Surviving spouse or domestic partner</strong></li>
<li><strong>Children</strong></li>
<li><strong>Grandchildren</strong></li>
<li><strong>Other heirs</strong> (parents, siblings, etc.)</li>
<li><strong>Public administrator</strong></li>
<li><strong>Creditors</strong> (after 45 days)</li>
<li><strong>Any other person</strong> (county resident)</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Multiple people can petition</strong> - court determines who should serve.</p>
<h2>Required Information and Documents</h2>
<h3>Before Filing, Gather:</h3>
<p><strong>About the Decedent:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Full legal name and any aliases</li>
<li>Last residence address</li>
<li>Date and place of death</li>
<li>Date of birth or age at death</li>
<li>Social Security number</li>
<li>Citizenship status</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Death Certificate:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Certified copy from county health department</li>
<li>Original or certified copy required for filing</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>The Will (if one exists):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Original will</li>
<li>All codicils (amendments to will)</li>
<li>Self-proving affidavit (if attached to will)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>About the Estate:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>List of all assets and approximate values</li>
<li>Real property addresses and APNs</li>
<li>Bank account information</li>
<li>Description of personal property</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>About Family Members:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Names and addresses of all heirs</li>
<li>Names and addresses of all will beneficiaries</li>
<li>Ages of any minor children</li>
<li>Relationship to decedent</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>About Proposed Executor/Administrator:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Full name and address</li>
<li>Relationship to decedent</li>
<li>Why qualified to serve</li>
</ul>
<h2>Completing Form DE-111: Step-by-Step</h2>
<h3>Header Section</h3>
<p><strong>Attorney Information:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Your attorney's name, State Bar number, address</li>
<li>Or "In Pro Per" if representing yourself</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Court Information:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Superior Court of California</li>
<li>County where decedent resided</li>
<li>Street address of courthouse</li>
</ul>
<h3>Item 1: Publication Information</h3>
<p><strong>Check box:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Will be published in (name of newspaper)</li>
<li>Located in (city), California</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Purpose:</strong> Notice to creditors and interested parties</p>
<p><strong>Usually:</strong> Newspaper of general circulation in county</p>
<h3>Item 2: Petitioner Information</h3>
<p><strong>Petitioner requests:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Check box for "Decedent's will and codicils, if any, be admitted to probate"</li>
<li>Full name be appointed as executor (if will) or administrator (if no will)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Executor vs. Administrator:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Executor:</strong> Named in will</li>
<li><strong>Administrator:</strong> No will or will doesn't name executor</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>With full authority</strong> (check box to request IAEA authority)</p>
<h3>Item 3: Decedent Information</h3>
<p><strong>a. Decedent's name:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Full legal name</li>
<li>"a.k.a." (also known as) if used other names</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>b. Date of death and age</strong></p>
<p><strong>c. Place of death</strong> (city, state)</p>
<p><strong>d. Residence at death:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Street address (required for jurisdiction)</li>
<li>City, State, ZIP</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>e. Registered domestic partner</strong> (check box if applicable)</p>
<h3>Item 4: Decedent's Property</h3>
<p><strong>a. Character and value:</strong></p>
<p>Check appropriate boxes and state values:</p>
<p><strong>Community/Quasi-community property:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Real property value: $__________</li>
<li>Personal property value: $__________</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Separate property:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Real property value: $__________</li>
<li>Personal property value: $__________</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Total:</strong> $__________</p>
<p><strong>These are estimates</strong> - formal inventory comes later.</p>
<p><strong>Purpose:</strong> Filing fees based on estate value.</p>
<p><strong>b. Non-probate property:</strong></p>
<p><strong>State value of property NOT going through probate:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Trust property</li>
<li>Joint tenancy property</li>
<li>Life insurance to named beneficiaries</li>
<li>Retirement accounts with beneficiaries</li>
<li>POD/TOD accounts</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Why report this:</strong> Court wants complete picture, though these assets don't go through probate.</p>
<h3>Item 5: Will Information</h3>
<p><strong>If will exists:</strong></p>
<p><strong>a. Date of will</strong> (month, day, year)</p>
<p><strong>b. Number of pages</strong> (including codicils)</p>
<p><strong>c. Codicils:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Check box if codicils exist</li>
<li>State dates and page counts</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>d. Self-proving will:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Check box if self-proving affidavit attached</li>
<li>Allows will admission without witness testimony</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>e. Lost will:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Check box if original will cannot be located</li>
<li>Requires special procedures and evidence</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>If no will:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Check box "There is no will"</li>
<li>Petition for Letters of Administration (not Letters Testamentary)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Item 6: Executor/Administrator Information</h3>
<p><strong>a. Appointment requested for:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Full name of proposed executor/administrator</li>
<li>Relationship to decedent</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>b. Nominee qualifications:</strong></p>
<p>Check all that apply:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Named as executor in will</strong> (if applicable)</li>
<li><strong>Related to decedent</strong> (state relationship)</li>
<li><strong>Principal beneficiary</strong> (inherits substantial share)</li>
<li><strong>Other interested person</strong> (explain)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>c. Nominee is:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Resident of California</strong> (preferred) OR</li>
<li><strong>Nonresident of California</strong> (check box)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Nonresidents:</strong> Must have California co-administrator</p>
<h3>Item 7: Priority and Waiver</h3>
<p><strong>a. Priority:</strong></p>
<p>State basis for appointment:</p>
<ul>
<li>Named in will</li>
<li>Surviving spouse</li>
<li>Highest priority heir</li>
<li>Other reason</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>b. Persons entitled to priority:</strong></p>
<p><strong>List people with equal or higher priority</strong> who are not petitioning:</p>
<ul>
<li>Names and relationships</li>
<li>Address (if known) or "whereabouts unknown"</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>c. Waiver:</strong></p>
<p><strong>Check boxes if:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>‚ñ° Waivers of persons with priority attached</li>
<li>‚ñ° Consent to appointment attached</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Waivers helpful but not required.</strong></p>
<h3>Item 8: Special Notice</h3>
<p><strong>Duty to give notice to certain people:</strong></p>
<p><strong>Probate Code Section 1250 notice required for:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Director of Health Care Services (if decedent received Medi-Cal)</li>
<li>Director of Mental Health (if applicable)</li>
<li>Certain</li>
</ul>
<p>government agencies</p>
<p><strong>Check boxes as applicable.</strong></p>
<h3>Item 9: Request for Special Orders</h3>
<p><strong>Optional requests:</strong></p>
<p><strong>IAEA Authority (most important):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>‚ñ° Full authority under IAEA</li>
<li>Allows executor to act without ongoing court approval</li>
<li>Highly recommended</li>
<li>Requires bond waiver in will OR beneficiary consent OR posting bond</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Other orders:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>‚ñ° Bond not required (if will waives bond)</li>
<li>‚ñ° Specified bond amount</li>
<li>‚ñ° Fees per Probate Code Section 10810</li>
<li>‚ñ° Appointment of probate referee</li>
</ul>
<h3>Item 10: Attachments</h3>
<p><strong>Required attachments:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>‚ñ° Original will and codicils</li>
<li>‚ñ° Typed copy of will</li>
<li>‚ñ° Probate Code Section 1250 notices</li>
<li>‚ñ° Waivers or consents</li>
<li>‚ñ° Other relevant documents</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Number of pages total:</strong> ______</p>
<h3>Signature</h3>
<p><strong>Declaration under penalty of perjury:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Petitioner (or attorney) signs and dates</li>
<li>Verification that statements are true</li>
<li>Under California law</li>
</ul>
<h2>Supporting Documents Required</h2>
<p><strong>File with Petition for Probate:</strong></p>
<p><strong>1. Original Will</strong> (if exists)</p>
<ul>
<li>Must file original</li>
<li>Photocopies not sufficient</li>
<li>If lost will, special procedures required</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>2. Typed Copy of Will</strong> (if handwritten or hard to read)</p>
<ul>
<li>Word-for-word transcription</li>
<li>Makes reading easier for court and parties</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>3. Certified Death Certificate</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Original or certified copy</li>
<li>Issued by county health department</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>4. Notice of Petition to Administer Estate (Form DE-121)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Notice to be published and mailed</li>
<li>Generated based on petition information</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>5. Duties and Liabilities of Personal Representative (Form DE-147)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Acknowledgment of executor duties</li>
<li>Must be signed by proposed executor</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>6. Order for Probate (Form DE-140)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Proposed order for judge to sign</li>
<li>Prepared by petitioner's attorney</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>7. Letters Testamentary/Administration (Form DE-150)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Template for letters court will issue</li>
<li>Shows executor's authority</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>8. Confidential Supplement (if minor beneficiaries)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Birth dates of minor children</li>
<li>Kept confidential</li>
</ul>

<li>111 N. Hill Street, Room 113</li>
<li>Los Angeles, CA 90012</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Other LA County courthouses</strong> may also accept filings.</p>

<h3>What Happens After Filing</h3>
<p><strong>1. Court reviews petition</strong> (1-2 weeks)</p>
<ul>
<li>Checks for completeness</li>
<li>Verifies filing fee paid</li>
<li>Assigns case number</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>2. Hearing date scheduled</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Typically 6-8 weeks after filing</li>
<li>Date, time, department noted on petition copy</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>3. Court returns conformed copies</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Shows case number and hearing date</li>
<li>Stamped by clerk</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>4. Petitioner must publish and mail notice</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Before hearing (specific timeline)</li>
<li>Proof of service filed with court</li>
</ul>
<h2>Notice Requirements</h2>
<p><strong>California Probate Code Sections 8110-8121</strong></p>
<h3>Notice of Petition Must Be:</h3>
<p><strong>1. Published</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Once a week for 3 consecutive weeks</li>
<li>In newspaper of general circulation</li>
<li>At least 15 days before hearing</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>2. Mailed</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>At least 15 days before hearing</li>
<li>To all heirs and beneficiaries</li>
<li>To anyone requesting special notice</li>
<li>First class mail</li>
</ul>
<h3>Proof of Service</h3>
<p><strong>Must file before hearing:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Proof of Publication (from newspaper)</li>
<li>Proof of Mailing (Form DE-120)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Without proper notice:</strong> Court will continue hearing to allow proper notice.</p>
<h2>The Probate Hearing</h2>
<h3>What to Expect</h3>
<p><strong>Appearance:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Petitioner (or attorney) appears</li>
<li>In assigned probate department</li>
<li>At scheduled date and time</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Typical hearing:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Calendar call (judge calls case name)</li>
<li>Brief questions from judge</li>
<li>Review of petition and will</li>
<li>Any objections heard</li>
<li>Decision announced</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>If no issues:</strong> Hearing takes 5-10 minutes</p>
<h3>Possible Outcomes</h3>
<p><strong>1. Petition Granted (most common)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Will admitted to probate</li>
<li>Executor/administrator appointed</li>
<li>Orders signed</li>
<li>Letters issued</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>2. Petition Continued</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Technical defects need correction</li>
<li>Additional information needed</li>
<li>Notice issues</li>
<li>New hearing date set</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>3. Petition Denied (rare)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Will invalid</li>
<li>Petitioner not qualified</li>
<li>Procedural problems</li>
</ul>
<h3>After Hearing</h3>
<p><strong>If granted:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Pick up signed orders and letters</li>
<li>Order certified copies (5-10)</li>
<li>Begin probate administration</li>
<li>File inventory within 4 months</li>
</ul>
<h2>Common Problems and Solutions</h2>
<h3>Problem: Missing Information</h3>
<p><strong>Solution:</strong> File corrected petition or provide information at hearing</p>
<h3>Problem: Will Cannot Be Found</h3>
<p><strong>Solution:</strong> File petition for lost will with evidence of will's contents and proper execution</p>
<h3>Problem: Someone Objects</h3>
<p><strong>Solution:</strong> Hearing becomes contested - may require separate trial</p>
<h3>Problem: Multiple People Want to Be Executor</h3>
<p><strong>Solution:</strong> Court determines who is most qualified based on priority and circumstances</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>How long after death should I file?</h3>
<p>No strict deadline, but sooner is better. Within 30 days typical. Delays can harm estate (property taxes, insurance, maintenance).</p>
<h3>Can I file without an attorney?</h3>
<p>Yes, but not recommended. Probate is complex and errors can be costly. Most people hire probate attorney.</p>
<h3>What if I make a mistake on the petition?</h3>
<p>Can file amended petition or correct at hearing if minor error. Serious errors may require re-filing.</p>
<h3>What if nobody wants to be executor?</h3>
<p>Public Administrator will be appointed to handle estate. Not ideal but available as last resort.</p>
<h3>How much does it cost to file?</h3>
<p>Filing fee $435-$935 based on estate value. Attorney fees additional (typically $3,000-$10,000 to prepare and file petition).</p>
<h3>What if decedent had property in multiple counties?</h3>
<p>File in county of residence. One probate handles property in all California counties.</p>
