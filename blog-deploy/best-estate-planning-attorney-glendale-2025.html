What Makes a Great Estate Planning Attorney?

Not all attorneys are equal when it comes to estate planning. Here's what distinguishes the best from the rest:

1. Focused Practice in Estate Planning

Estate planning is a specialized area of law. An attorney who dabbles in estate planning while primarily practicing other types of law may not have the depth of knowledge you need.

Look for:

  • Estate planning as a primary practice area
  • Experience with California-specific laws and procedures
  • Familiarity with local county recording offices and requirements
  • Ongoing education in estate planning developments

2. Experience That Matters

Years of experience alone don't guarantee quality, but they do provide valuable perspective. An experienced attorney has seen a wide range of family situations, knows how plans fail, and understands how to draft documents that actually work.

Questions to ask:

  • How long have you practiced estate planning specifically?
  • How many living trusts have you drafted?
  • Have you dealt with situations similar to mine?
  • Have you handled trust administration and probate (so you understand what happens when plans succeed or fail)?

3. Transparent Pricing

One of the biggest frustrations people have with attorneys is not knowing what things cost. A great estate planning attorney will tell you upfront exactly what you'll pay and what's included.

Red flags:

  • Refusing to discuss pricing until after consultation
  • Vague answers about "it depends"
  • Hidden fees revealed after you've started
  • Pressure to upgrade to more expensive packages

What you should expect:

  • Clear package pricing with itemized inclusions
  • Upfront disclosure of any additional costs
  • Written fee agreements before work begins

4. Comprehensive Service

A living trust is just one piece of a complete estate plan. The best attorneys provide a comprehensive package that includes everything you need.

A complete estate plan typically includes:

  • Revocable living trust
  • Pour-over will
  • Financial power of attorney
  • Healthcare power of attorney
  • HIPAA authorization
  • Deed transfer for your home (critical!)
  • Trust funding guidance for other assets

5. Personal Attention

Estate planning is deeply personal. You're making decisions about your family, your assets, and what happens after you're gone. You deserve an attorney who takes time to understand your situation and goals.

Look for:

  • Attorneys who ask thoughtful questions about your family
  • Willingness to explain things until you understand
  • Availability to answer questions during the process
  • Direct access to the attorney (not just staff)

Questions to Ask Before Hiring an Estate Planning Attorney

Before you hire any attorney, ask these critical questions:

Experience Questions

  1. How long have you practiced estate planning in California specifically? (Look for significant experience with California law)
  2. Is estate planning your primary practice area? (Should be a major focus, not a sideline)
  3. Have you handled situations similar to mine? (Blended families, business owners, special needs, etc.)
  4. Do you also handle trust administration and probate? (Understanding what happens later informs better planning)

Pricing Questions

  1. What is your total fee for a living trust package? (Get a complete number, not a range)
  2. What exactly is included in that price? (Get a detailed list)
  3. Are there any additional costs I should know about? (Recording fees, additional deeds, etc.)
  4. Do you prepare and record the deed to transfer my home? (This should be included)
  5. What do updates or amendments cost? (Life changes; know the ongoing costs)

Process Questions

  1. Will I work directly with you, or with staff? (Understand who's actually doing the work)
  2. How long does the process take? (Typically 2-4 weeks for a living trust)
  3. Will you explain the documents to me? (You should understand what you're signing)
  4. What happens after I sign? (Ensure proper funding and follow-up)

Relationship Questions

  1. Do you offer a free initial consultation? (Common and helps you assess fit)
  2. How can I reach you if I have questions later? (Ongoing availability matters)
  3. Will you be available to help my family after I pass? (Trust administration support)

Red Flags to Watch For

In my 25+ years of practice, I've heard stories about attorneys who didn't serve their clients well. Here are warning signs:

Pricing Red Flags

  • Refusing to discuss pricing: Any attorney who won't tell you costs upfront is hiding something
  • Bait-and-switch pricing: Low initial quote that grows with add-ons
  • Significantly below-market pricing: If it's too cheap, they're cutting corners somewhere
  • Pressure to buy expensive packages: Good attorneys recommend what you need, not what maximizes their fees

Sales Red Flags

  • High-pressure tactics: "You must sign today" or "This price expires tomorrow"
  • Fear-based selling: Excessive scary stories to push you toward expensive options
  • Pushing unnecessary products: Life insurance, annuities, or investments (attorneys shouldn't be selling these)
  • Seminar sales pitches: Free dinner seminars often lead to high-pressure sales

Quality Red Flags

  • No California State Bar number: All California attorneys have a Bar number‚Äîverify it
  • Unwillingness to explain: If they can't or won't explain what you're getting, that's a problem
  • Template-only approach: One-size-fits-all documents without customization
  • No deed transfer service: If they don't handle the deed, your trust won't work
  • Rush to sign: Quality planning takes time for discussion and drafting

Communication Red Flags

  • Hard to reach: If they're difficult to contact before you hire them, it'll only get worse
  • Dismissive of questions: Your questions are valid; they should be answered patiently
  • Jargon without explanation: Good attorneys explain things in plain English
