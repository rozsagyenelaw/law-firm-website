The Reality of LA County Probate: A System in Crisis

Los Angeles County has the largest population of any county in California—over 10 million residents. Yet all probate matters for this massive population funnel through a single location: the Stanley Mosk Courthouse at 111 North Hill Street in downtown Los Angeles.

The Numbers Tell the Story

Consider these sobering statistics about LA County probate:

  • 55,000+ pending cases: At any given time, the Stanley Mosk Courthouse probate division has over 55,000 active cases
  • Limited judicial resources: A handful of probate judges handle this massive caseload
  • Hearing wait times: Initial hearing dates are often scheduled 6-8 weeks out, and subsequent hearings can take even longer
  • Staffing challenges: Court staff are stretched thin, leading to delays in processing documents and orders

The COVID-19 pandemic made everything worse. Courts closed or operated at reduced capacity for extended periods. The backlog that existed before 2020 multiplied. Years later, the system is still trying to catch up.

What This Means for Glendale Families

When someone passes away in Glendale without a living trust, their family faces a stark reality: even a "simple" estate with a single home and basic bank accounts will likely take 18-24 months to settle through probate. Complex estates—those with multiple properties, business interests, or family disputes—can take 3-5 years or longer.

During this entire time:

  • The family home cannot be sold without court approval
  • Bank accounts may be frozen
  • Investment accounts cannot be accessed
  • Bills, property taxes, and maintenance costs continue to accumulate
  • Family members may face financial hardship while waiting for their inheritance

The True Cost of Probate for Glendale Homeowners

Let's talk about money. California is one of the few states that sets probate fees by statute, meaning the fees are fixed by law based on the gross value of the estate. These fees apply to both the attorney and the executor (personal representative).

California Statutory Probate Fee Schedule

Estate Value Fee Percentage
First $100,000 4%
Next $100,000 3%
Next $800,000 2%
Next $9,000,000 1%
Above $10,000,000 0.5%

Critical point: These fees are based on gross estate value, not net. If your Glendale home is worth $1.2 million but you still owe $400,000 on the mortgage, fees are calculated on the full $1.2 million—not the $800,000 in equity.

What Actually Happens at Stanley Mosk Courthouse

Let me walk you through what your family will experience if your estate goes through probate at Stanley Mosk Courthouse.

The Initial Filing

After you pass away, someone (usually a family member named as executor in your will, or a family member petitioning to serve as administrator if there's no will) must file a Petition for Probate at Stanley Mosk Courthouse.

The courthouse is located in downtown Los Angeles at 111 North Hill Street. Parking is expensive and limited. The building is old and crowded. Your family member will navigate security lines, find the correct courtroom, file the petition, and pay filing fees.

The first hearing is typically scheduled 6-8 weeks after filing. During this time, legal notices must be published in a newspaper and mailed to all heirs and beneficiaries.

The Hearing Calendar

On the day of the hearing, your family member must travel to downtown LA, find parking, clear security, and wait in a crowded courtroom. Probate calendars are often packed with dozens of cases. Your family member might wait hours for a hearing that lasts just a few minutes.

If something is missing from the paperwork—and something often is—the hearing will be continued to another date, weeks or months away. The whole process starts over.

The Creditor Period

After the executor is appointed, a mandatory 4-month creditor period begins. Creditors can file claims against the estate. This period cannot be shortened. Your family waits.

The Inventory and Appraisal

Within 4 months of appointment, the executor must file an inventory and appraisal of all estate assets. A probate referee (court-appointed appraiser) values real property. More fees, more waiting.

Ongoing Administration

Throughout the process, the executor must:

  • Manage estate assets responsibly
  • Pay ongoing bills and expenses
  • File income tax returns for the decedent and the estate
  • Keep detailed records of all transactions
  • Respond to court orders and requests

If the family home needs to be sold, additional court approval is often required. This can add months to the process.

Final Accounting and Distribution

When everything is complete, the executor files a final accounting with the court—a detailed record of every asset, expense, and transaction. The court reviews this accounting (more waiting), and if approved, authorizes final distribution to beneficiaries.

Only then—often 18-24 months after the death—can your family finally receive their inheritance.

The Simple Solution: A Living Trust

All of this—the courthouse visits, the waiting, the fees, the stress—can be avoided with proper planning. A revocable living trust allows your assets to pass directly to your beneficiaries without any court involvement.

How a Living Trust Works

When you create a living trust:

  1. You transfer ownership of your assets (home, bank accounts, investments) to the trust
  2. You remain in control as the trustee during your lifetime—you can buy, sell, or change anything
  3. You name a successor trustee to take over when you pass away
  4. When you die, your successor trustee distributes assets according to your instructions—no court required

The process typically takes 4-8 weeks instead of 18-24 months. There are no statutory probate fees. No courthouse visits. No public record of your assets and beneficiaries. For more information, consult a Pasadena probate attorney.
