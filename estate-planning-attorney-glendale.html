What is Estate Planning?

Estate planning is the process of creating legal documents and strategies to ensure your assets are protected during your lifetime and distributed according to your wishes after your death. A comprehensive estate plan does much more than just transfer property - it protects your family from probate court, minimizes taxes, provides for healthcare decisions if you become incapacitated, and ensures your legacy is preserved.

For Glendale residents, estate planning is particularly important due to high California property values. With the median home value in Glendale at approximately $740,000, most families need a living trust to avoid the costly probate process. As experienced estate planning attorneys in Glendale, we create customized estate plans that protect your assets, minimize taxes, and provide peace of mind for you and your family.

Whether you're a young family with minor children, a homeowner looking to avoid probate, or approaching retirement with significant assets, proper estate planning is essential. Without an estate plan, California law decides who inherits your property, who makes medical decisions if you're incapacitated, and your family must endure the expensive probate process.

Common Estate Planning Mistakes Glendale Residents Make

As experienced Glendale estate planning attorneys, we've seen these costly mistakes time and again. Avoid these common errors:

Don't Make These Estate Planning Mistakes

  • Procrastinating: "I'll do it later" often means it never gets done. Unexpected illness or death leaves your family facing probate court and difficult decisions.
  • Using Online DIY Forms: Generic online trusts often have errors, aren't properly executed, or don't comply with California law. We regularly see families in probate court because their DIY trust was invalid.
  • Forgetting to Fund the Trust: Creating a trust but not transferring assets into it is like buying a safe but leaving your valuables on the table. Unfunded trusts don't avoid probate.
  • Not Updating After Major Life Events: Marriage, divorce, children, property purchases, and other life changes require estate plan updates. Old plans can cause unintended consequences.
  • Failing to Plan for Incapacity: Many people focus on death planning but forget about incapacity. Without powers of attorney, your family faces expensive conservatorship proceedings.
  • Inadequate Beneficiary Designations: Life insurance, retirement accounts, and bank accounts with outdated or missing beneficiaries can create family conflicts and tax problems.

Our experienced Glendale estate planning attorneys ensure your plan is properly prepared, executed, and funded to avoid these costly mistakes.

What's included in an estate plan?

A comprehensive estate plan includes several key documents: (1) A revocable living trust that holds your assets and avoids probate, (2) A pour-over will that transfers any assets outside the trust into it, (3) Durable power of attorney for financial matters if you become incapacitated, (4) Advance healthcare directive expressing your medical wishes, (5) HIPAA authorization allowing family to access health information, and (6) For parents, guardian nominations for minor children. We also provide guidance on funding your trust by transferring assets into it.

The best time to create an estate plan is now. Major life events that trigger the need for estate planning include: purchasing a home (especially in expensive markets like Glendale), getting married or divorced, having children, inheriting property, starting a business, or approaching retirement. However, unexpected illness or accidents can happen at any age. Young families need estate planning to name guardians for children and avoid conservatorship if incapacitated. How often should I update my estate plan?

Review your estate plan every 3-5 years or after major life events.
