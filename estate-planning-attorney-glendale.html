Estate Planning FAQs

How much does estate planning cost in Glendale?

This includes the living trust, pour-over will, power of attorney, healthcare directive, and trust funding assistance. Complex estates or those requiring special needs trusts may cost more. We provide transparent pricing during your free consultation.

What's the difference between a will and a living trust?

A will goes through probate court after death, which can take 6-18 months and cost 4-7% of your estate in fees. A living trust avoids probate entirely, allowing your successor trustee to distribute assets immediately and privately. Living trusts also provide incapacity protection, allowing someone to manage your affairs if you become unable to. For most California families, a living trust is the better choice.

Do I need an estate planning attorney or can I do it myself?

While DIY estate planning options exist, mistakes can be costly. Improperly drafted documents may fail when needed, leaving your family in probate court. An experienced estate planning attorney ensures documents are properly executed, trusts are correctly funded, and your plan works with California law. The cost of professional estate planning is far less than the cost of fixing mistakes or going through probate.

What documents do I need for a complete estate plan?

A complete California estate plan typically includes: a revocable living trust, a pour-over will, a durable power of attorney for finances, an advance healthcare directive, and HIPAA authorization. Depending on your situation, you may also need a special needs trust, irrevocable life insurance trust, or other specialized documents. Our Glendale estate planning lawyer will recommend the right documents for your needs.

How long does estate planning take?

Most estate plans can be completed within 2-3 weeks. The process includes an initial consultation to understand your goals, document drafting (usually 1-2 weeks), and a signing meeting. Trust funding, which involves transferring assets into your trust, may take additional time depending on the complexity of your estate. We work efficiently while ensuring everything is done correctly.

When should I update my estate plan?

You should review your estate plan every 3-5 years and update it after major life events such as marriage, divorce, birth of children or grandchildren, death of a beneficiary or trustee, significant changes in assets, moving to a new state, or changes in tax laws. Our firm offers estate plan reviews and updates to ensure your documents remain current and effective.
